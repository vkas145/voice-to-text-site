What is Speech Analytics?
How AI Reads Your Sales Calls

Speech analytics turns recorded conversations into structured, searchable, scoreable data. It is how modern teams understand what is actually being said on thousands of calls — without listening to each one.

Definition

Speech analytics is the technology that converts spoken audio into text, then analyses that text for meaning, emotion, keywords, compliance markers, and quality signals — automatically and at scale.

How Speech Analytics Works: The Three-Layer Process

1

Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR)

The audio is converted to text. This is the transcription layer — the accuracy of everything downstream depends on the quality of ASR. For Indian calls, this means the ASR must handle Hindi, Hinglish, Tamil, and other Indic languages accurately.

2

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

The text is analysed for meaning. NLP identifies entities (product names, prices, dates), emotions (frustration, satisfaction), topics, and intent. This is where keyword detection, sentiment analysis, and compliance checking happen.

3

Analytics and Scoring

The structured data from NLP is aggregated into scores, reports, and flags. Individual calls get quality scores; agent trends emerge; compliance violations surface. This layer turns data into decisions.

What Speech Analytics Can Detect

  • Keyword and phrase presence: Did the agent mention the free look period? Did the customer say "not interested"? Exact phrase detection at scale.
  • Emotional sentiment: Is the customer frustrated, confused, satisfied, or coerced? Detected from word choice, pacing, and language patterns.
  • Compliance gaps: Were required disclosures made? Were prohibited phrases used? Automatically checked on every call.
  • Talk/listen ratio: How much did the agent talk vs the customer? High agent talk time often predicts lower conversion.
  • Dead air and interruptions: Long silences, talking over the customer — patterns that indicate agent uncertainty or poor call flow.
  • Topic categorisation: Automatically tagging calls by what they were about — pricing, objections, competitor mentions, technical issues.

Speech Analytics vs Call Recording: Key Difference

Call recording captures audio. Speech analytics makes it useful. A recording sitting in a folder is evidence — you can retrieve it if something goes wrong. Speech analytics is intelligence — it tells you what is going wrong before complaints are filed.

Most contact centres have call recordings. Very few have speech analytics applied to them. The gap between having recordings and having insights is exactly where teams lose.

Use Cases for Speech Analytics in Indian Contact Centres

🏦

BFSI Compliance

Auto-detecting missing RBI/IRDA disclosures on loan and insurance sales calls before a Banking Ombudsman complaint is filed.

📈

Sales Coaching

Identifying which agents consistently miss buying signals or struggle with price objections — then coaching on the exact transcript moment.

😤

Churn Prevention

Flagging calls where customer frustration spikes — in retention teams, these are the calls that need a supervisor callback within 24 hours.

🔍

Competitive Intelligence

Tracking when customers mention competitor names across hundreds of calls — what triggers the comparison and how agents respond.

The Indic Language Challenge in Speech Analytics

Most speech analytics platforms were built on English-language training data. When applied to Indian call centres — where agents and customers move freely between Hindi, English, Tamil, Telugu, and regional languages — accuracy collapses. A system that misses 30% of words cannot reliably detect compliance violations or sentiment.

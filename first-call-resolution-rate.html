📊 Call Center Metric

First Call Resolution Rate:
The Metric That Matters Most

FCR measures whether customer issues are resolved in a single contact — no callbacks, no escalations, no repeat contacts. It is the most powerful predictor of both customer satisfaction and contact centre cost efficiency.

What is First Call Resolution (FCR)?

First Call Resolution (also called First Contact Resolution) is the percentage of customer calls in which the issue is fully resolved during the first interaction — without the customer needing to call back, follow up via another channel, or be transferred to a different department.

A customer who calls with a billing query and has it resolved in one call is an FCR. A customer who calls about the same billing query three times before it is fixed represents three calls — but zero FCR events.

FCR Formula
FCR = (Calls Resolved on First Contact ÷ Total Calls) × 100

A 1% improvement in FCR typically reduces operating costs by approximately 1% — because every repeat call is an incremental cost. For a 100-agent contact centre handling 5,000 calls per day, moving FCR from 70% to 75% means 250 fewer repeat calls per day.

The Business Impact of Low FCR

  • Direct cost: Every repeat contact for the same issue doubles the handling cost for that customer's problem.
  • Customer churn: Customers who experience repeat contacts for the same issue are significantly more likely to churn. Telecom and banking data consistently show a 3x higher churn rate for customers with 3+ contacts for the same issue.
  • Agent morale: Agents handling the same unresolved issues repeatedly burn out faster. FCR is a leading indicator of agent attrition.
  • Escalation volume: Unresolved first contacts often escalate to supervisors, complaints, or regulators — each more costly than the original call.

Why FCR Drops: The 5 Most Common Causes

Agent Knowledge Gaps

The agent does not know the answer and either gives incorrect information (leading to a repeat call) or transfers the customer unnecessarily. Call QA on FCR-failed calls reveals which knowledge gaps are most common.

Insufficient Authority to Resolve

The agent knows the solution but cannot implement it without manager approval. Waiver limits, refund thresholds, and plan change permissions that are too restrictive force unnecessary escalations.

Process Complexity

The resolution process requires steps that cannot all be completed in one call — document submission, verification delays, multi-department approvals. FCR fails that are process-driven need process redesign, not agent coaching.

Incomplete Issue Identification

The agent resolved the stated issue but missed a related underlying issue that the customer calls back about the next day. AI analysis of FCR-failed call transcripts shows what secondary issues were not probed.

Inaccurate Information Given

The customer received incorrect information on the first call — wrong timelines, wrong amounts, wrong process steps. This is a call quality failure detectable through transcript review of repeat callers' first contacts.

How AI Call Auditing Improves FCR

The most effective way to improve FCR is to audit the calls that failed — specifically the first call for customers who called back within 7 days for the same issue. Transcript analysis of these calls reveals exactly where the first interaction broke down.

Without AI, reviewing these calls manually is time-intensive. With call transcription and AI analysis, a QA manager can review 20 to 30 FCR-failed calls in an hour and identify the 3 to 4 systemic failure patterns driving the issue.

Script Compliance Monitoring:
Know Who Follows the Script

You trained your agents. You approved the script. But do you know how many calls follow it? AI script compliance monitoring checks every call against your required disclosures and script elements — automatically.

What Is Script Compliance Monitoring?

Script compliance monitoring is the process of verifying that agents on customer calls cover all required elements — disclosures, legal statements, product information — in the approved manner. Traditionally done through manual spot-checks, AI makes it possible to check every call.

There are two types of script compliance:

  • Mandatory compliance: Legally required disclosures (e.g., interest rate in APR terms, free look period for insurance, recording disclosure) that must appear on every applicable call.
  • Quality compliance: Preferred script elements (opening format, needs discovery questions, objection handling phrases) that improve conversion and consistency but are not legally mandated.

Both types can be monitored by AI, but they are treated differently in the audit output — violations of mandatory compliance are flagged as high-risk, while quality compliance gaps become coaching inputs.

How AI Checks Script Compliance on a Call

After a call is transcribed, the AI analyses the transcript for the presence, absence, and accuracy of specified script elements. This works in three ways:

Keyword and Phrase Detection

The most straightforward approach: the AI checks whether required phrases appear in the transcript. "Yeh call quality ke liye record ki ja rahi hai" — if this phrase or its semantic equivalent does not appear within the first 60 seconds, the call is flagged as a recording disclosure miss.

Semantic Equivalence Checking

Agents naturally vary their phrasing. "Processing fee 2% lagegi" and "Do percent fee cut hoga" and "Ek chota sa processing charge hai" all communicate the same information in different ways. Semantic checking catches disclosures made in varied language, reducing false positives compared to exact-match keyword checking.

Sequence and Placement Analysis

Some disclosures must occur at specific points in the call — not just anywhere. A recording disclosure at the end of a call is not compliant. AI can check whether required elements appear in the correct sequence and within appropriate time windows.

Compliance Score Distribution — What Teams Typically Find

When teams first run AI script compliance monitoring, the initial distribution typically looks like this:

35%
Full compliance — all mandatory elements present
42%

23%

These numbers improve significantly within 30 days once agents know their calls are being monitored. The improvement is not primarily from management action — it is from agents self-correcting when they know every call is reviewed.

Why Convert Hindi Voice Notes to Text?

Voice notes are convenient to send but inconvenient to consume. You can't search them, skim them, or refer back to a specific detail without replaying the entire message. Here are the most common reasons people convert Hindi voice notes to text:

  • You're in a meeting or public place and can't play audio out loud
  • The voice note is long (2-5 minutes) and you need the key information quickly
  • You need to forward the content as text to someone who doesn't speak Hindi
  • You want a written record of instructions, addresses, or important details
  • You're a journalist or researcher who received an interview or source recording via WhatsApp
  • Accessibility you're deaf or hard of hearing and need the text equivalent

How to Convert Hindi WhatsApp Voice Notes to Text

Step 1: Download the voice note from WhatsApp

On Android: Long-press the voice message in WhatsApp, tap the three-dot menu, and select "Share". Save it to your phone's Files or Downloads folder. The file will be in .opus format Bolo Aur Likho handles this natively.

On iPhone: Long-press the voice message, tap "Forward", then share it to "Save to Files". You can also tap the share icon and save directly.

Step 2: Upload to Bolo Aur Likho

Go to boloaurlikho.com and upload your voice note file. No signup, no account creation just upload and go.

Step 3: Select Hindi and transcribe

Choose "Hindi" from the language dropdown. If your voice note is in Hinglish (mixed Hindi-English), Hindi mode handles this perfectly. Click "Transcribe Now".

Step 4: Get your text in seconds

Your Hindi voice note will be converted to Devanagari text within seconds. You can copy the text, download it, or share it directly. If you enabled AI Summary, you'll also get a concise summary of the key points.

Handling Hinglish Voice Notes

Most Indians don't speak pure Hindi in WhatsApp voice notes they speak Hinglish, mixing Hindi and English naturally. Bolo Aur Likho handles this seamlessly. Hindi words appear in Devanagari script, English words appear in Latin script. The result is a natural, readable transcript that mirrors how the person actually spoke.

For example, a voice note saying "मैंने उसको email भेजा है, please check कर लो" will be transcribed exactly like that with the Hindi in Devanagari and the English words in English. No awkward transliteration, no confusion.

Tips for Better Accuracy

  • Select Hindi manually instead of using auto-detect for best results
  • Quiet environment matters voice notes recorded in noisy environments (auto, market) may have lower accuracy
  • Longer is fine even 5-10 minute voice notes work. They're processed in parallel chunks
  • Multiple speakers work group voice notes with multiple Hindi speakers are handled well

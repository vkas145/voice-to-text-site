What Call Sentiment Analysis Actually Detects

Sentiment analysis on customer calls goes far beyond "positive" and "negative". A well-trained model identifies distinct emotional states throughout the conversation, mapped to specific moments in the transcript. Here is what it surfaces:

😤

Customer Frustration

Detected through raised voice, repeated questions, phrases like "main pehle bhi bata chuka hoon" or "aap sun nahi rahe". Flags calls that need immediate follow-up.

😕

Confusion and Uncertainty

Long pauses, clarifying questions, hedging language like "lekin mujhe pata nahi" indicate a customer who has not understood the product. Conversion is unlikely without more information.

😊

Positive Engagement

Affirmative responses, questions about next steps, and forward-looking language ("toh kab se start hoga?") signal a customer moving toward a decision.

😰

Pressure and Discomfort

Short responses, monosyllabic answers, and phrases like "theek hai, okay, haan haan" without genuine engagement may indicate a coerced sale — a compliance risk in regulated industries.

How Sentiment Analysis Works on Hindi and Hinglish Calls

Sentiment analysis on Indian sales calls is more complex than English-only tools can handle. Indian agents and customers frequently switch between Hindi, English, and regional languages mid-sentence. Emotional expression in Hindi uses different idioms, intensity markers, and indirect communication styles that a purely English-trained model misses.

Bolo Aur Likho's sentiment analysis is built on top of OpenAI Whisper and processes transcripts in their native language, preserving Hinglish expressions and their emotional context rather than forcing translation first.

Practical Uses for Sales Managers

  • Churn prediction: Customers who sound frustrated on retention calls are far more likely to churn. Flagging these calls for immediate supervisor callback can save the relationship.
  • Conversion coaching: Agents whose calls show high confusion in the middle of the pitch need product knowledge training, not a different closing technique.
  • Compliance risk identification: Calls where customer sentiment shifts negative after a pricing or disclosure discussion may indicate mis-selling language was used.
  • Recognition: Identifying calls with consistently positive customer sentiment helps you understand what your top performers are doing differently.

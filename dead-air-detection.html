🔇 Dead Air Detection

Dead Air Detection:
Silence That Signals a Problem

A few seconds of silence on a call is natural. Five seconds of silence while a customer waits is awkward. Fifteen seconds of dead air while an agent searches for information is a call quality failure. AI detects exactly when and why silence happens.

What is Dead Air on a Call?

Dead air refers to extended periods of silence during a call where neither the agent nor the customer is speaking. Unlike natural conversational pauses (1 to 3 seconds), dead air typically lasts 5 seconds or more and signals a problem — the agent is searching for information, navigating a slow system, unsure of the answer, or lost in the conversation flow.

Dead air is distinguished from hold time. When an agent explicitly places a customer on hold with a message, the customer expects silence. When silence happens without explanation during an active conversation, customer satisfaction drops sharply — within as little as 8 to 10 seconds, customers begin to wonder if the call has disconnected.

Dead Air Thresholds: What Each Duration Signals

1–3 sec
Natural conversational pause. Normal. Not flagged.
4–7 sec
Agent is thinking or checking something. Acceptable but worth monitoring for frequency.
8–15 sec
Agent is searching for information. Customer patience thinning. Should be acknowledged verbally ("main check karta hoon, ek second").
15+ sec
Significant dead air. High risk of customer disengagement or call abandonment. Flagged as a quality issue.

The 5 Causes of Dead Air

Knowledge Gap

Agent Does Not Know the Answer

The customer asks a question the agent cannot answer immediately — product features, pricing details, policy terms. The agent goes silent while searching documentation or a knowledge base. Fix: better training and faster-access knowledge bases.

System Delay

Slow CRM or Internal Tools

The agent is waiting for a screen to load, a record to pull up, or a system to respond. The customer hears silence. Fix: technology and process, not coaching — agents cannot speak faster than their systems.

Script Navigation

Agent Lost in Script or Call Flow

Newer agents frequently fall silent while reading ahead in a script or trying to find the right section of a call guide. Fix: script simplification and script memorisation training.

Objection Shock

Unexpected Objection With No Prepared Response

A customer raises an objection the agent was not expecting and goes silent while thinking of a response. Extended silence here signals the agent needs more objection handling preparation.

After-Call Prep

Agent Completing Notes During the Call

Some agents write call notes or update CRM records while still technically on the call, creating silent periods the customer experiences as dead air. Fix: post-call note automation.

How AI Detects Dead Air

In a call transcript, dead air appears as extended gaps between speaker turns — periods where neither the [Agent] nor [Customer] label is active. AI identifies these gaps, measures their duration, and flags instances above the defined threshold (typically 8 seconds) as quality issues.

At scale, dead air data reveals systemic patterns: which agents have the most dead air, at what point in calls it occurs, and whether it correlates with specific call types, objection categories, or product queries. This turns a vague "agents hesitate" impression into specific, actionable data.

A weighted scoring template covering the six dimensions of call quality — with a version for general sales calls and a compliance-weighted version for BFSI teams. Use it manually or let AI score it automatically.

A call QA scorecard is only useful if it is weighted to reflect what actually matters. A call where an agent is friendly but misrepresents the product is not a good call — but a flat 10-point-per-dimension score might rate it 8/10. Weighted scoring puts more points on the dimensions that have the most business and compliance impact.

Customising the Scorecard for BFSI

For insurance, banking, and NBFC sales calls, the compliance section should be weighted higher — typically 35 to 40% of the total score. The specific compliance items depend on your regulator:

  • Insurance (IRDA): Free look period, product classification, exclusion disclosure, premium obligation
  • Banking/NBFC (RBI): APR disclosure, processing fee, prepayment charges, collection timing
  • Telecom (TRAI): Plan speed/data accuracy, auto-renewal disclosure, DND verification

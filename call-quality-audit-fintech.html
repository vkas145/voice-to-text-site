Fintech Companies

Fintech companies scale fast and add human call teams for activation, support, and collections. These calls carry regulatory obligations that many young fintech operations are not yet equipped to monitor at scale — creating compounding compliance risk.

Which Fintech Call Teams Need Quality Auditing?

Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL)

Activation calls, limit increase upsells, and payment reminder calls — all with RBI Fair Practices Code obligations on disclosure and collection conduct.

Neobanks and Payment Apps

KYC calls, account opening support, card activation, and fraud alert calls — compliance with RBI's digital lending guidelines and customer consent requirements.

Wealth and Investment Platforms

Onboarding calls for mutual fund or stock investment accounts carry SEBI's KYC and risk profiling requirements — all must be captured and verified.

Credit Score and Lending Platforms

Loan eligibility calls, disbursement follow-ups, and repayment reminder calls — RBI digital lending guidelines since 2022 significantly increased disclosure requirements.

🚨 RBI's Digital Lending Guidelines (September 2022) and subsequent circulars require that all key fact statements and loan terms be communicated verbally and/or in writing at the point of sanction. Call recordings are the primary evidence of verbal compliance.

The Four High-Risk Call Patterns in Fintech

BNPL Activation Without Interest Rate Disclosure

Many BNPL activation calls focus entirely on the ease and speed of activation — never stating the effective interest rate on the credit facility. RBI requires disclosure of Annual Percentage Rate (APR) in clear terms. This omission is increasingly penalised.

Limit Increase Upsells Without Consent Documentation

An agent calling to say "sir, aapki limit badha di gayi hai" — presenting a limit increase as a fait accompli rather than seeking consent — is a consent violation. The customer must actively agree to the increased limit and its terms.

Investment Product Calls Without Risk Profiling

SEBI's suitability requirements mean an agent cannot recommend a mutual fund or stock without establishing the customer's risk profile. Calls where product recommendations precede any risk or financial situation questions are SEBI violations.

Third-Party Collections Without Proper Identification

If a third-party collection agency is calling on behalf of the fintech, RBI requires clear identification of the lending institution and the purpose of the call. Generic collection calls that do not identify the original lender are non-compliant.

Why Fintech Teams Struggle With Call QA

Most fintech operations scale their call teams fast — hiring 50 to 200 agents in months — without scaling their QA capacity. The QA processes that worked for a 10-person team do not work for 100 agents making 3,000 calls per day. Manual QA falls behind, compliance gaps accumulate, and the first signal of the problem is a regulatory notice or a media story.
